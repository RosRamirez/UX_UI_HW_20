Project Hawaii

A web and mobile redesign

Project Overview

Project Hawaii, a non-profit aiding the homeless, has upgraded its website for a better volunteer experience. The new design simplifies task flow, making it easy for volunteers to find mission information, browse opportunities, and sign up based on their skills and interests. This tech-driven revamp showcases the organization's commitment to serving the community and improving lives.

Research

As a group we interviewed 5 people that had volunteered at a homelessness non-profit before. I interviewed 1 of those people. After consolidating our notes into an affinity map, we found that our users had common goals in:

Fullfillment

Feel motivated and happy to support a cause they believe in

Community

Want to help give back to those in need within their local spaces

Busy Schedule

Would like to volunteer more but can't seem to find events that work with their schedule

Limited Resources

Money is tight so they prefer to find other ways to support

Problem Statement

How can we redesign our website so upcoming volunteer opportunities are organized and users are able to feasibly sign up to these events despite their limited schedule?

Project Overview

This case study highlights the importance of user-centered design in redesigning the Project Hawaii website. By conducting comprehensive user research, incorporating feedback, and iterating on wireframes, we created a responsive and user-friendly design. The streamlined navigation and improved content accessibility cater to the needs of volunteers, enhancing their engagement. User testing played a vital role in identifying and addressing usability issues, while collaboration with stakeholders ensured alignment with project goals. This case study emphasizes the significance of continuous evaluation and refinement to deliver a high-quality user experience.
